About CaribHealth Foundation

A Connecticut 501(c)(3) charitable organization, incorporated in 2024 to facilitate and promote access to high-quality medical care across the Caribbean — with a focus on underserved and indigent populations.

Our Mission

Reducing disparities in specialty care.

CaribHealth Foundation exists to close a measurable gap: across much of the Caribbean, conditions routinely managed in higher-resource settings go undiagnosed or untreated for years. Our model focuses on direct clinical service, the equipment to sustain it, and the training and grants that build lasting local capacity.

The Foundation grew from the clinical observations of its founder, gastroenterologist Dr. Gyanprakash Ketwaroo, during work in the region. It was incorporated in Connecticut in March 2024 and recognized as a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization the same year.

We are a small organization by design — keeping administrative costs minimal so that resources reach patients and partner providers.

Our Principles

What guides our work.

01 Patient dignity

Every patient is entitled to respectful, evidence-based care regardless of geography or means.

02 Local partnership

We work alongside local clinicians and community health workers. Our role is to support and strengthen, not to replace.

03 Financial transparency

Program outcomes and financials are made available to the public. Donor trust is foundational to our work.

04 Evidence-based practice

Our clinical and educational programs follow established standards of care and are continuously evaluated.

Our programs

Four interlocking programs — defined in our charter — turn contributions into direct care, durable infrastructure, and a stronger local health workforce across the Caribbean.

Program 01

Medical Missions

Direct, hands-on care at partner sites.

Volunteer clinical teams travel to partner sites across the Caribbean to deliver screenings, consultations, and procedures — with founding strength in digestive health and endoscopy. Every mission pairs visiting specialists with local providers so that knowledge stays in the community after the team departs.

GI screening & endoscopy Consultations Bedside teaching
Program 02

Equipment Donation

The tools that make care possible.

We source, refurbish, and ship endoscopic and diagnostic equipment, medications, and supplies to clinics operating with limited infrastructure. On-site installation and orientation ensure that donated equipment is used safely and stays in service.

Endoscopy systems Diagnostics Consumables
Program 03

Training & Education

Building lasting local capacity.

Workshops and continuing education equip local clinicians and community health workers to recognize, triage, and refer digestive and other conditions earlier. Training is delivered alongside missions and as standalone sessions, with curricula adapted to each partner site.

Symptom recognition Triage & referral Procedure technique
Program 04

Training Grants

Investing in the next generation.

Grants support the education and training of medical professionals who serve — or are preparing to serve — communities across the Caribbean. By funding the people who stay, we extend the impact of every mission far beyond a single visit.

Continuing education Specialty training Caribbean-wide

Governance & Stewardship

How We Govern

Officers

The board elects a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer to lead day-to-day governance.

Meetings

Directors convene at least three times per year, with a quorum of two required to act.

Committees

An Executive Committee and advisory committees support specific programs and initiatives.

Oversight

The Treasurer maintains financial records and reports regularly to the board for review.

Board Membership
